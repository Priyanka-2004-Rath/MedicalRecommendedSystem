Building a Symptom-Based Disease Diagnosis Web App with Flask and Machine Learning

In the age of technology and information, access to accurate and timely healthcare is more critical than ever. With the increasing importance of remote healthcare solutions, we embarked on a journey to develop a symptom-based disease diagnosis web application. Leveraging Flask for the backend and a Support Vector Machine (SVM) model, we created a user-friendly platform that can help users identify potential illnesses based on their reported symptoms.

The Problem

The project began with recognizing a common issue: people often experience symptoms and want quick answers about their health concerns. It can be challenging to differentiate between various diseases, especially when symptoms overlap. Our goal was to provide a convenient solution for users to input their symptoms and receive potential diagnoses along with comprehensive health recommendations.

The Solution

We developed a web app that allows users to enter a list of symptoms they are experiencing. The app then uses a pre-trained Support Vector Machine (SVM) model to predict the most likely disease based on the provided symptoms. Here's how it works:

  1. Symptom Input: Users enter their symptoms through a user-friendly interface. The web app supports a wide range of 132 different symptoms, making it versatile for different scenarios.
  2. Machine Learning Model: We trained multiple models including SVM, Random Forest, Gradient Boosting, KNeighbors, and Naive Bayes using a comprehensive dataset containing 4,920 records of symptoms and corresponding diseases. The SVM model achieved 100% accuracy and was selected as our final model.
  3. Disease Prediction: The app uses the SVM model to predict the most likely disease from 41 different conditions, providing users with instant information about potential health concerns.
  4. Comprehensive Recommendations: To enhance user experience, the app provides detailed information about the predicted disease, including descriptions, recommended precautions, medications, dietary advice, and workout tips.

Technical Implementation

Dataset: Our model was trained on a dataset with 4,920 samples and 133 features (132 symptoms + 1 target disease)

Model Performance: All tested models (SVM, Random Forest, Gradient Boosting, KNeighbors, MultinomialNB) achieved 100% accuracy

Final Model: Support Vector Machine with linear kernel, saved as 'svc.pkl' for deployment

Key Features

  • User-Friendly Interface: The web app boasts an intuitive and easy-to-navigate interface, ensuring that users can input their symptoms without any hassle.
  • Accurate Predictions: Leveraging machine learning, our SVM model provides 100% accurate disease predictions based on the symptoms provided by the user.
  • Comprehensive Information: In addition to predictions, the app offers comprehensive information about the predicted disease, including descriptions, precautions, medications, diet recommendations, and workout tips.
  • Educational Content: To empower users with knowledge, the app includes informative content about various health topics and AI-powered healthcare solutions.
  • Multiple Disease Coverage: The system can diagnose 41 different diseases ranging from common conditions like fungal infections to more complex ones like hepatitis variants.

Conclusion

Our Symptom-Based Disease Diagnosis Web App brings the power of machine learning and healthcare information to the fingertips of users. It serves as a valuable resource for individuals looking to gain insights into their health conditions quickly and conveniently. By combining advanced SVM technology with comprehensive healthcare databases, we aim to make healthcare more accessible and user-centric.

In an era where remote healthcare solutions are gaining prominence, this project represents a significant step forward in providing accessible and reliable healthcare information to the masses. With 100% model accuracy and comprehensive health recommendations, we hope that this project will contribute to better health awareness and ultimately improve the well-being of individuals worldwide.
